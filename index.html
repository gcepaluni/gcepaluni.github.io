Gabriel Cepaluni

Political Scientist · gabriel.cepaluni@unesp.br

I am an Associate Professor in the School of Humanities and Social Sciences at São Paulo State University (UNESP). I hold a Ph.D. in Political Science from the University of São Paulo, and a Master's degree in International Relations from the San Tiago Dantas Program (UNESP, UNICAMP and PUC-SP). I obtained my Habilitation (or Livre-Docência) at the International Relations Institute at the University of São Paulo.

I study forced labor, political inequality, and the political economy of development, with a focus on Brazil and Latin America. Using causal inference methods and large administrative datasets, my research asks when and why states protect — or fail to protect — the most vulnerable. I am the author of Capitalism and Development: Steering Markets Toward Fairness (Cambridge University Press), and my work has appeared in American Journal of Political Science, Political Analysis, World Development, European Journal of Political Economy, and Nature.
