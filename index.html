Gabriel Cepaluni

Associate Professor · Political Science
gabriel.cepaluni@unesp.br

I am an Associate Professor in the School of Humanities and Social Sciences at São Paulo State University (UNESP). I hold a Ph.D. in Political Science and Habilitation (Livre-Docência) from the University of São Paulo. My research focuses on comparative political economy and public policy, especially in developing countries.

My work combines experimental, quasi-experimental, and mixed-methods approaches to understand racial bias, income inequality, child labor, the welfare state, and political institutions. I have published in leading journals, including American Journal of Political Science, World Development, and Political Analysis, with over 2,400 Google Scholar citations as of July 2025.

I have held visiting fellowships at institutions such as Notre Dame, Central European University, NYU, Brown, Georgetown, and UNU-WIDER, and have secured competitive research funding from FAPESP, Mellon-LASA, Erasmus+, and the Institute for Humane Studies. My book manuscript is under contract with Cambridge University Press.

In addition to research, I serve as a reviewer for leading journals (APSR, AJPS, JOP, etc.), have organized international workshops, and contributed as an evaluator for national dissertation prizes. I teach and supervise in both English and Portuguese, and my work reaches audiences across Latin America, Europe, and Asia.
