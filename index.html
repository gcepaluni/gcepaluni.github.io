Gabriel Cepaluni

Political Scientist · gabriel.cepaluni@unesp.br

I am an Associate Professor in the School of Humanities and Social Sciences at São Paulo State University (UNESP). I hold a Ph.D. in Political Science from the University of São Paulo, and a Master's degree in International Relations from the San Tiago Dantas Program. I obtained my Habilitation (Livre-Docência) at the International Relations Institute at the University of São Paulo.

My research lies in comparative political economy, with a substantive focus on developing countries. I study how institutions shape inequality, labor coercion, and state capacity, using experimental and quasi-experimental methods applied to large-scale administrative and novel datasets. My work combines causal inference strategies with data-intensive approaches, including the use of computational methods and text-based data.

My research has appeared in leading journals such as American Journal of Political Science, Political Analysis, World Development, European Journal of Political Economy, International Political Science Review, and Nature. I am also the author of Capitalism and Development: Steering Markets Toward Fairness (Cambridge University Press).

I have held visiting research positions at the University of Notre Dame and the United Nations University (UNU-WIDER), and have teaching and supervision experience in both Brazil and the United Kingdom.
