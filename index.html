Gabriel Cepaluni

Political Scientist · gabriel.cepaluni@unesp.br

I am an Associate Professor in the School of Humanities and Social Sciences at São Paulo State University (UNESP). I hold a Ph.D. in Political Science from the University of São Paulo, and a Master's degree in International Relations from the San Tiago Dantas Program. I obtained my Habilitation (Livre-Docência) at the International Relations Institute at the University of São Paulo.

My research is in comparative political economy, with a focus on developing countries. I study how institutions shape inequality, labor coercion, and state capacity using experimental and quasi-experimental methods applied to large administrative datasets. My work combines causal inference strategies with data-intensive approaches, including computational methods and text analysis.

My research has appeared in leading journals such as American Journal of Political Science (2x), Political Analysis, World Development, European Journal of Political Economy, International Political Science Review, and Nature. I am also the author of Capitalism and Development: Steering Markets Toward Fairness (Cambridge University Press).

I have held visiting positions at the University of Notre Dame and the United Nations University (UNU-WIDER), and have taught and supervised students in Brazil and the United Kingdom.
