Gabriel Cepaluni

I am an Associate Professor at UNESP (São Paulo State University). I study forced labor, political inequality, and the political economy of development, with a focus on Brazil and Latin America. Using causal inference methods and large administrative datasets, my research asks when and why states protect — or fail to protect — the most vulnerable. I am the author of Capitalism and Development: Steering Markets Toward Fairness (Cambridge University Press), and my work has appeared in American Journal of Political Science, Political Analysis, World Development, European Journal of Political Economy, and Nature.
